\section{Sampling}
\label{sec:sampling}

Sampling asks for expectations under a target law \(\pi\), not just one good point.
Exact independent sampling is often unavailable, so the problem is to design estimators and transition mechanisms that still approximate those expectations reliably.
Stable probability calculations, proposal geometry, and gradient information remain central throughout, whether the method is i.i.d.\ Monte Carlo or MCMC.
The recurring objects are the target law \(\pi\), proposal objects \(q\), and transition kernels \(K\).
