\subsection{Geometry and conditioning}
\label{subsec:conditioning}

The next issue is not whether a direction is locally correct, but how the surrounding geometry scales that direction.
Condition numbers summarize how anisotropic the local quadratic model is, and preconditioning changes the geometry of the update to compensate.
These same ideas about geometry control return later in gradient-based MCMC, HMC, and diffusion models.

Preconditioning still asks us to choose a metric from outside the objective.
Second-order methods push the same idea one step further by building the metric from the local Hessian itself.
That shift turns every update into a structured linear-algebra problem, which is the next layer of the optimization story.
