\subsection{Local models and convexity}
\label{subsec:optimization-euclidean}

Much of modern statistics and machine learning can be phrased as choosing a parameter vector $x\in\R^d$ to minimize an objective.
To decide how to move from a current point, we first need a local model of how the objective changes nearby.
Gradients record first-order change, Hessians record curvature, and convexity and smoothness inequalities tell us when these local objects control behavior away from the current point.
We begin with these Euclidean definitions and then turn them into descent guarantees that will drive the algorithms in the rest of the unit.
